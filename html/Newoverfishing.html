What is Overfishing?

What happens is that fishing industries use fishing nets with massive weights dragging it across the ocean for profit. Of course, this process is very efficient compared to say spearfishing but the environmental damage this has caused is unimaginable. On the floor, sea beds have been ruined, and seaweed, kelp and molluscs have been demolished and ripped from the ground.

Problem

The most common fish that is being overfished in the pacific ocean is tuna. Many different types of tuna exist yet no matter the type, their meat is considered nutrient-rich and filled with omega-3, the key ingredient to make cell walls in the human body and keep the blood healthy. Although this is a vital substance for the human body, the economic fueling that has been given to overfishing fisheries is enormously bad.

Every year, billions of dollars are spent investing in fisheries and aquaculture. It is estimated that one fishing net worth of fish is around $3 million US dollars in the states, so it’s a very profitable investment for many people, but with that, the greed of taking millions of fish before they are even grown has increased. Solutions

Donate to help fund work in under-resourced regions. The Marine Stewardship Council is on a mission to end overfishing and ensure fish forever. By donating to the MSC either at an individual or corporate level, you can help us further our mission and engage more fisheries around the world. These allow small-scale fisheries and fisheries in the developing world to improve their environmental performance in a stepwise approach.

Share real news.
