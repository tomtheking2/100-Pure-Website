What is Coral Bleaching

Coral bleaching is when water is too warm, and corals reject the algae living in their tissues, causing the coral to turn completely white. Algae and coral depend on each other, without algae the coral wouldn't have its vibrant color or have a source of food. Water runoff and pollution which can contain pollutants, overexposure to sunlight and extreme low tides. As little as a 0.1 degree celsius change can drive out algae.

Problem

25% of marine animals would lose their habitat. Many species of wild life need the coral for their diet. Without coral they wouldn't be able to protect from themselves from predators, this also won't be able to sustain the fishing and tourism industries. Their disappearance will therefore have economic, social and health consequences. Coral reefs protect coastlines from storms and erosion, provide jobs for local communities, and offer opportunities for recreation.

Coral bleaching impacts people's, food security, and safety. Coral reefs are natural barriers that absorb the force of waves and storm surges, keeping coastal communities safe. Without them, we must rely on man made sea walls that are expensive, less effective, and environmentally damaging to construct.

Solutions

The main cause of coral bleaching is because of global warming and people worldwide littering on a daily basis. Here are some of the main solutions to fixing coral bleaching. - safe/responsible diving and snorkeling: Avoid touching reefs or anchoring your boat on the reef. Contact with the reef will damage the delicate coral animals, and anchoring on the reef can kill corals, so look for sandy bottom


- Recycle and dispose of trash properly.
- Minimize use of fertilizers.
- Reduce stormwater runoff.
- Save energy at home and at work.
- Be conscious when buying aquarium fish.
